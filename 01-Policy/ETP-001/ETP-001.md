# ETP-001 — Enterprise Technology Policy

**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** Executive Draft for Technical Writing and Approval  
**Policy Owner:** Chief Information Officer  
**Policy Steward:** Office of the CIO  
**Responsible Organization:** Enterprise Architecture  
**Review Frequency:** At least annually and upon material regulatory, organizational, risk, operating-model, or technology change

## Executive Summary

The Bank shall maintain an integrated Enterprise Technology Governance Framework that establishes executive accountability, enterprise architecture authority, technology standards governance, lifecycle governance, control traceability, evidence requirements, exception management, and regulatory alignment across the complete technology lifecycle.

The Policy governs. Enterprise Technology Standards implement. Procedures execute. Engineering standards, reference architectures, configuration standards, implementation guides, and runbooks define technical implementation.

## 1. Purpose

The Bank shall establish enterprise-wide requirements for the governance, design, acquisition, development, implementation, operation, maintenance, monitoring, resilience, security, modernization, and retirement of technology assets and technology-enabled services.

## 2. Scope

The Bank shall apply this Policy to all enterprise technology, including applications, software, SaaS, cloud services, infrastructure, networks, databases, APIs, integration platforms, data platforms, artificial intelligence, end-user technology, security technology, development environments, production environments, disaster recovery environments, managed services, contractors, affiliates, and third parties.

## 3. Regulatory and Control Alignment

The Bank shall maintain technology governance aligned with applicable FFIEC guidance, FDIC expectations, Federal Reserve expectations, OCC expectations where applicable, NIST Cybersecurity Framework 2.0, NIST SP 800-53 Rev. 5, CIS Controls v8, COBIT 2019, ISO/IEC 27001, ITIL 4, SOX technology control obligations, internal risk appetite, and audit or remediation commitments.

The Bank shall maintain traceability among policy requirements, standards, procedures, control objectives, controls, evidence, exceptions, issues, accountable owners, systems of record, and regulatory obligations.

## 4. Governance Authority

### 4.1 Chief Information Officer

The Chief Information Officer shall own this Policy and remain accountable for the effectiveness of Enterprise Technology Governance.

### 4.2 Office of the CIO

The Office of the CIO shall administer the governance framework, coordinate policy lifecycle management, maintain executive reporting, coordinate governance forums, support regulatory remediation, and maintain governance records.

### 4.3 Enterprise Architecture

Enterprise Architecture shall govern enterprise technology architecture, Enterprise Technology Principles, Enterprise Technology Standards, technology domains, reference architectures, Architecture Decision Records, technology lifecycle, technology modernization, technical debt, and the Architecture Review Council.

### 4.4 Chief Security Officer

The Chief Security Officer shall govern cybersecurity, information security, physical security, identity and access management, security architecture, security operations, cryptographic standards, and security requirements.

### 4.5 Technology Risk

Technology Risk shall provide independent Second Line oversight, credible challenge, control governance, exception review, risk reporting, and examination support.

### 4.6 Internal Audit

Internal Audit shall provide independent Third Line assurance over governance design and operating effectiveness.

## 5. Governance Bodies

The Bank shall execute technology governance through the following bodies:

- Board Risk Committee
- Executive Risk Committee
- Executive Technology Council
- Architecture Review Council
- Technology Risk Committee
- Change Advisory Board

The Architecture Review Council shall remain the authoritative enterprise technology architecture governance forum. Its operating process shall be defined by the approved Architecture Review Council Procedure.

The Change Advisory Board shall govern production implementation readiness and shall not replace required Architecture Review Council approval.

## 7. Enterprise Technology Standards Governance

The Bank shall maintain a controlled Enterprise Technology Standards Catalog.

Each standard shall identify its owner, accountable executive, responsible organization, scope, applicability, review cadence, related policy, principles, control objectives, evidence requirements, systems of record, supporting procedures, regulatory mappings, lifecycle state, and approval authority.

The Bank shall review standards at least annually and upon material regulatory, technology, architecture, security, audit, or organizational change.

## 8. Enterprise Architecture Governance

The Bank shall maintain Enterprise Architecture as the design authority for material technology decisions.

Enterprise Architecture shall maintain:

- Business capability models
- Technology domain models
- Enterprise reference architectures
- Architecture Decision Records
- Architecture Review Council records
- Technology roadmaps
- Technology lifecycle catalogs
- Technology currency registers
- Technical debt registers
- Application portfolio relationships
- Enterprise Technology Taxonomy

Material technology initiatives shall complete required Architecture Review Council governance before implementation. The approved Architecture Review Council Procedure shall define intake, Preliminary Solution Review, Engineering Design Review, Solution Delivery Review, exceptions, approvals, and evidence retention.

## 9. Technology Decision Governance

The Bank shall classify technology decisions according to strategic impact, business impact, architecture significance, security impact, risk, operational complexity, lifecycle impact, and regulatory obligations.

Material decisions shall produce documented evidence, including applicable standards, principles, Architecture Decision Records, risk assessments, security reviews, approvals, exceptions, production-readiness evidence, and implementation evidence.

## 10. Technology Lifecycle Governance

The Bank shall govern technology from strategy through retirement.

Lifecycle governance shall include:

- Strategy and demand
- Business capability alignment
- Standards applicability
- Architecture review
- Security review
- Technology Risk review
- Engineering and testing
- Operational readiness
- Change approval
- Production operation
- Monitoring and optimization
- Technology currency review
- Technical debt review
- Modernization
- Retirement

Technology shall be classified using approved lifecycle states such as Strategic, Current, Containment, Sunset, and Retired.

## 11. Information Security and Technology Protection

The Bank shall integrate security, privacy, resilience, identity, cryptography, data protection, physical security, monitoring, and incident-management requirements throughout the technology lifecycle.

Detailed implementation requirements shall remain in approved security policies, standards, procedures, and reference architectures.

## 12. Technology Risk Integration

The Bank shall operate Enterprise Technology Governance under the Three Lines Model.

First Line technology organizations shall own risk and execute controls. Technology Risk shall provide independent Second Line oversight and challenge. Internal Audit shall provide independent Third Line assurance.

## 13. Enterprise Technology Controls Framework

The Bank shall maintain an Enterprise Technology Controls Framework with the following hierarchy:

Policy Requirement → Control Objective → Enterprise Control → Control Activity → Evidence → Test Procedure → Regulatory Mapping.

Each control shall have a unique identifier, owner, accountable executive, control type, automation level, execution frequency, testing method, evidence source, system of record, standard reference, and regulatory mapping.

## 14. Evidence Management

The Bank shall retain evidence sufficient to demonstrate governance implementation and operating effectiveness.

Evidence shall include, as applicable:

- Policy and standards approvals
- Governance meeting minutes
- Architecture Review Council records
- Architecture Decision Records
- Standards applicability assessments
- Risk and security assessments
- Exception and risk-acceptance records
- Change approvals
- Testing results
- Technology inventories
- CMDB and CSDM relationships
- Application portfolio records
- Lifecycle and currency assessments
- Technical debt reports
- Attestations
- Metrics and executive reporting

## 15. Exceptions and Risk Acceptance

The Bank shall manage deviations from policy, standards, architecture, controls, or required governance through an approved exception process.

Each exception shall identify the impacted requirement, business justification, risk, accountable owner, compensating controls, remediation plan, expiration date, residual risk, approval authority, and evidence.

Exceptions without expiration dates shall not be permitted.

## 16. Compliance Monitoring and Reporting

The Bank shall maintain sustainable compliance monitoring and executive reporting.

Reporting shall include, as applicable:

- Policy and standards review status
- Standards ownership
- Architecture Review Council throughput
- Architecture compliance
- Exception volume and age
- Unsupported technology
- End-of-life exposure
- Technical debt
- Technology currency
- Modernization progress
- Evidence completeness
- Control testing status
- Audit and regulatory findings

## 18. BIAN Business Capability Alignment

The Bank shall maintain its authoritative business capability model aligned to the BIAN Service Landscape.

Initial enterprise capability domains include Customer and Channel Management, Consumer Deposits, Consumer Lending, Commercial Banking, Payments and Cards, Treasury Management, Wealth Management, Risk and Compliance, Finance and Performance, and Enterprise Support.

The detailed capability model shall use the licensed BIAN model and approved BIAN service-domain decomposition.

## 19. ServiceNow CSDM 5.0 and APM Alignment

The Bank shall align Application Portfolio Management and service modeling to ServiceNow CSDM 5.0 out-of-the-box constructs.

The approved relationship is:

BIAN-aligned Business Capability → Business Application → Business Service → Application Service → Technical Service / Service Offering → Configuration Item → Infrastructure.

No governance artifact shall introduce a conflicting application or service hierarchy without Architecture Review Council approval.

## 20. Policy Administration

The Bank shall review this Policy at least annually and following material regulatory change, organizational change, technology-strategy change, audit observation, examination finding, or governance-model change.

Material changes shall follow the approved policy governance process and receive required executive approval before publication.
