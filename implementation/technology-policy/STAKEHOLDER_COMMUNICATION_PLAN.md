# Technology Policy Stakeholder Communication Plan

## Purpose

This plan defines how the final Technology Policy publication baseline is communicated, adopted, and reinforced across Enterprise Architecture, Technology Risk, Engineering, Operations, Security, Product, and platform ownership teams.

## Communication Objectives

1. Establish the Technology Policy baseline as the authoritative governance source.
2. Clarify that policy governs, standards implement, procedures execute, and engineering standards configure.
3. Reinforce that ARB approval is required before material technology change implementation.
4. Explain how requirements, controls, evidence, RTM, exceptions, and lifecycle governance will operate.
5. Prevent uncontrolled local interpretations of standards, patterns, and control expectations.

## Audience Segments

| Audience | Message Focus | Expected Action |
|---|---|---|
| CIO / Executive Leadership | Governance posture, regulatory alignment, operating risk reduction | Endorse and monitor adoption |
| Enterprise Architecture | Ownership of policy framework, standards, ARB, registers, and patterns | Operate and maintain baseline |
| Technology Risk / Compliance | Control traceability, evidence, exceptions, and reporting | Align control testing and evidence model |
| Engineering / Product Teams | Design-time expectations, ARB triggers, evidence requirements | Use standards and submit required artifacts |
| Technology Operations | CMDB/CSDM alignment, lifecycle risk, resilience evidence | Execute operational control evidence |
| Security Architecture | Secure-by-design, IAM, data protection, threat review | Enforce security architecture gates |
| Platform Owners | ServiceNow, Archer/IRM, ADO/Jira integration and import disposition | Implement or formally defer platform mapping |

## Core Message

The Technology Policy framework is now the authoritative control plane for technology governance. It connects policy, standards, principles, patterns, requirements, controls, evidence, regulatory traceability, ARB decisioning, lifecycle management, exceptions, and implementation tooling into one governed operating model.

## Communication Sequence

| Step | Communication | Owner | Timing | Evidence |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Executive publication notice | EA Leader | Day 0-5 | Announcement record |
| 2 | EA and architecture community briefing | EA Leader | Day 0-15 | Meeting notes / attendance |
| 3 | Engineering and product enablement session | EA + Engineering Leads | Day 15-30 | Deck / recording / FAQ |
| 4 | Technology Risk and Compliance alignment | EA + Risk | Day 15-30 | Control mapping confirmation |
| 5 | ServiceNow / Archer / ADO-Jira platform owner review | EA + Platform Owners | Day 30-60 | Disposition log |
| 6 | Monthly governance reporting activation | EA + Risk | Day 60-90 | Dashboard / report |

## Required Communication Artifacts

- Publication notice
- Executive summary
- ARB usage guide
- Standards overview
- Requirements/control/evidence primer
- Exception and waiver guide
- 30/60/90 adoption plan
- FAQ
- Monthly adoption scorecard

## FAQ Topics

1. When is ARB required?
2. What is a material technology change?
3. What evidence is required for architecture approval?
4. How are exceptions approved and tracked?
5. Where do requirements and controls live?
6. How does this align to ServiceNow, Archer/IRM, and ADO/Jira?
7. What happens if teams bypass the policy baseline?
8. How are lifecycle risks escalated?

## Success Criteria

- All accountable owner groups are briefed.
- ARB intake references the published baseline.
- Technology Risk recognizes the control/evidence model.
- Platform owners formally disposition implementation mappings.
- Monthly adoption scorecard is operational.
- Exceptions and waivers are routed through the approved process.

## Anti-Patterns

- Publishing without stakeholder enablement.
- Treating the repository as an archival document store.
- Allowing local teams to redefine requirement or control IDs.
- Running ARB outside the documented decision model.
- Treating tool import deferrals as permanent exemptions.

## Status

Initial communication plan established for post-publication implementation.
